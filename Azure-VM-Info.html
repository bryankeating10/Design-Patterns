Important Considerations

The free offer covers VM compute hours, but other services can incur costs. Understanding these details is key to keeping your bill at zero.

The VM is free, but attached resources might not be.

Be mindful of the options you select during setup. To avoid charges, you must stick to free-tier eligible options for services like:

  • Storage Disks: Standard HDDs or certain small Standard SSDs are often included, but Premium SSDs will incur costs.
  • Public IP Addresses: Dynamic IPs are usually free, but static IPs typically have a small hourly charge.
  • Data Transfer: While you get a free monthly allowance for outbound data, exceeding it will lead to charges.

"Stopped" is not the same as "Deallocated".

To truly stop the billing clock and your usage of free hours, you must deallocate the VM. Simply shutting down the operating system from inside the VM is not enough.

Stopped (Billing Continues)

Shutting down the OS keeps resources like the IP address allocated to you, and the clock keeps ticking.

Stopped (Deallocated) - BILLING STOPS

Using the "Stop" button in the Azure portal releases the compute resources, and billing for the VM stops.

Free services are subject to regional availability.

While Azure has a global presence, not all VM types are available in every region. When creating your virtual machine, ensure that the B1s, B2pts v2, or B2ats v2 series is available in your chosen location. You can deploy your free VMs in any region where they are supported.
